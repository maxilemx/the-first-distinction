# The First Distinction

**A map of intuition put to the test**
*On co-emergence, the role of intuition, and why self-reference may have no solitary origin*

## What this is

This repository records an exploration into one old, unresolved question: **who or what draws the first distinction?** If everything we perceive is a distinction (this / not-this, inside / outside, self / world), the very first one cannot have been drawn by something that already distinguished — that leads to infinite regress.

The document maps this question against the thinkers who have worked on it (Spencer-Brown, Luhmann, Varela, Nāgārjuna, Rovelli, Tononi), separates what is already resolved from what remains genuinely open, and isolates one hypothesis worth developing:

> There may be no solitary "first distinction." The minimum that can exist is not a *one* that distinguishes itself from nothing, but a **minimal pair that co-emerges** — where each term has identity only in relation to the other. Self-reference without infinite regress may require a pair that co-defines itself (as DNA's two strands copy each other), never a solitary origin.

This is **not a claim of discovery.** It is a well-posed open question, together with what it would take to turn it into a defensible contribution: an operational definition, a testable prediction, and a falsification condition.
